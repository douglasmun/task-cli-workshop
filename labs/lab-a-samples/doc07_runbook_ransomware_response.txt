SOC Runbook — Ransomware Alert Response

When a ransomware alert fires, the responding analyst must act immediately to limit encryption spread while preserving forensic evidence. Upon receiving a ransomware-related detection, begin by confirming the alert is not a false positive. Corroborate with endpoint telemetry: look for mass file rename events, shadow copy deletion commands (vssadmin delete shadows), and the creation of ransom note files in multiple directories. If two or more of these indicators are confirmed, treat the incident as a confirmed ransomware event and escalate to Severity 1.

The first containment action is network isolation of the affected host. Initiate an automated or manual network quarantine through your EDR platform or switch VLAN isolation, cutting the host from lateral movement paths while retaining management-plane connectivity for investigation. Do not power off the host at this stage; volatile memory may contain encryption keys recoverable by forensic tools. Simultaneously, identify accounts that have authenticated from the affected host in the preceding 48 hours and suspend those accounts pending investigation.

Conduct a rapid blast radius assessment by querying your SIEM for the same indicators on adjacent hosts and shared file server access logs. Ransomware frequently pre-stages on multiple hosts before triggering encryption simultaneously; discovery within the first 15 minutes can prevent a single-host incident from becoming an enterprise-wide event. Check for evidence of data exfiltration preceding the encryption event, including large outbound transfers to cloud storage endpoints or uncommon IP addresses.

For eradication, restore affected systems from the last known-good backup taken prior to the initial access event, not just prior to encryption. The initial compromise typically predates encryption by days or weeks. Validate restored systems against a clean baseline before reconnecting to the network. Once contained, collect a full forensic image of the affected endpoint and preserve all relevant logs for root cause analysis and potential law enforcement engagement.
