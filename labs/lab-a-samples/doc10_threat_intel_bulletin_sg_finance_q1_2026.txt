Threat Intelligence Bulletin — Singapore Financial Sector Threat Landscape Q1 2026

During Q1 2026, the Singapore financial sector experienced a notable increase in targeted intrusion activity relative to the preceding quarter. This bulletin summarizes observed campaigns, actor attributions, and recommended defensive priorities for financial institutions operating under MAS regulatory oversight.

The most significant campaign cluster involved a threat actor assessed with moderate confidence as Jade Cicada (tracked alternatively as APT-SG41), targeting SGX-listed entities and at least two domestic banks with spear-phishing lures themed around MAS circular announcements and SWIFT payment advisory notifications. The lures delivered macro-enabled documents exploiting CVE-2025-21334 to achieve initial access on Windows endpoints. Post-compromise activity included deployment of the SILKTHREAD backdoor family and extended dwell times averaging 47 days before detection.

A separate financially motivated campaign targeted retail banking customers of three local banks through adversary-in-the-middle phishing infrastructure, harvesting one-time passwords and session tokens in real time to bypass SMS-based MFA. This campaign used Telegram bots as operator interfaces and leveraged Cloudflare Workers to proxy phishing pages, complicating takedown efforts. Approximately 1,200 customer accounts were confirmed compromised across the three institutions.

Ransomware activity remained below regional averages in the financial sector specifically, though two mid-tier insurance companies reported encrypted endpoints attributed to IronLock affiliates. Both incidents were contained at the workstation level without spread to core systems.

Defensive recommendations for Q1 2026 based on observed activity include: prioritizing patches for network edge appliances given continued exploitation of known CVEs as initial access vectors; implementing hardware security key MFA for all customer-facing banking applications to mitigate adversary-in-the-middle phishing; conducting threat-hunt exercises focused on scheduled task persistence and HTTPS beaconing with regular intervals, indicators consistent with SILKTHREAD implant activity; and reviewing third-party vendor access to SWIFT messaging infrastructure following intelligence suggesting reconnaissance activity against correspondent banking relationships in the ASEAN region.
